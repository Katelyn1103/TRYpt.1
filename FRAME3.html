DISTANCE LEARNING


Image source: https://www.philstar.com/headlines/2021/01/03/2067819/distance-learning-becomes-new-norm-philippines-education


Due to the Covid-19 pandemic, the school year 2020-2021 started late. The start of the school year was
pushed through a distance learning set-up due to the social distancing protocol of the government that
does not enable students to learn face to face. Different kinds of distance learning were implemented
such as the online, modular and the blender learning. Other private schools opened the school year
during the month of August but public schools moved the opening of class on October 5, 2021. There
was also approved pilot implementation of face-to-face classes in low-risk areas starting January stated
by the President, on December 15, 2020 but the approval was recalled five days after, on December 26,
2020, due to a threat of a new Covid-19 variant. The DepEd considers a two weeks summer vacation
plan to extend the school year and a lot of people wanted to reject this plan.

TYPHOON "AURING"


Image source:https://www.philstar.com/headlines/2021/02/22/2079598/auring-leaves-over-4500-passengers-stranded-affects-more-53k-persons


On February 17, 2021, a tropical depression near Mindanao has entered the Philippine Area of
Responsibility thus becoming the country’s first tropical cyclone for 2021. This storm named ”Auring”
(international name: Dujuan) was intensified into a tropical storm on February 18, 2021.

The next day, in the early morning of February 19, 2021, it intensified into a severe tropical storm but
weakened back to a tropical storm during the 5:00 pm bulletin of PAG-ASA on the same day. Three days
after, February 22, 2021, in its 2:00 pm bulletin, the storm weakened into a Low-Pressure Area.

It was expected to dissipate as it approaches Southern Luzon. The storm “Auring” has left more than
4,500 people stranded and has affected more than 53,000 people showed in the government data. The
former typhoon “Auring” has left the country in a report on February 24, 2021, and has caused damages
to our agriculture worth P21.43 million.

COVID-19 PANDEMIC


Image source:https://www.adb.org/sites/default/files/styles/content_media/public/content-media/91806-covid-19-response-philippines.jpg?itok=U558F2aw


COVID-19 is a new disease, caused by a novel (or new) coronavirus that has not
previously been seen in humans. Because it is a new virus, scientists are learning more
each day. Although most people who have COVID-19 have mild symptoms, COVID-19 can
also cause severe illness and even death. Some groups, including older adults and people
who have certain underlying medical conditions, are at increased risk of severe illness.

On February 11, 2020 the World Health Organization announced an official name for
the disease that is causing the 2019 novel coronavirus outbreak, first identified in Wuhan
China. The new name of this disease is coronavirus disease 2019, abbreviated as COVID-19.
In COVID-19, “CO” stands for corona, “VI” for virus, and ”D” for disease. Formerly, this
disease was referred to as “2019 novel coronavirus” or “2019-nCoV.”

COVID-19 is thought to spread mainly through close contact from person to person,
including between people who are physically near each other (within about 6 feet). People
who are infected but do not show symptoms can also spread the virus to others. Cases of
reinfection with COVID-19 have been reported but are rare. We are still learning about how
the virus spreads and the severity of illness it causes.

COVID-19 spreads very easily from person to person. How easily a virus spreads from
person to person can vary. The virus that causes COVID-19 appears to spread more
efficiently than influenza but not as efficiently as measles, which is among the most
contagious viruses known to affect people.

The virus can lead to pneumonia, respiratory failure, heart problems, liver
problems, septic shock, and death. Many COVID-19 complications may be caused by a
condition known as cytokine release syndrome or a cytokine storm. This is when an infection
an infection triggers your immune system to flood your bloodstream with inflammatory proteins called
cytokines. They can kill tissue and damage your organs.

Scientists and researchers are constantly tracking COVID-19 infections and recoveries.
But they don’t have information about the outcome of every infection. Early estimates predict
that the overall COVID-19 recovery rate will be between 97% and 99.75%.

There's no way to tell how long the pandemic will continue. There are many factors,
including researchers’ work to learn more about the virus, their search for a treatment, the
success of the vaccines, and the public’s efforts to slow the spread.
